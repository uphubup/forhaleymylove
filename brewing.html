Brewing Methods

Explore the art and science of brewing coffee. Each method brings out unique flavors and aromas. Here are some of the most popular ways to brew coffee.

French Press

Produces a full-bodied, rich cup. Steep coarse grounds in hot water, then press the plunger to separate grounds from coffee.

Espresso

Intense and concentrated. Hot water is forced through finely ground coffee at high pressure, creating a bold shot with crema.

Pour-Over

Clean and nuanced. Hot water is poured over coffee grounds in a filter, allowing for precise control over extraction.

AeroPress

Versatile and quick. Uses air pressure to push hot water through coffee, resulting in a smooth, rich cup.

Cold Brew

Steep coarse grounds in cold water for 12-24 hours. The result is a smooth, less acidic coffee served over ice.

Siphon

Theatrical and aromatic. Uses vacuum pressure and heat to brew coffee, producing a clean and complex cup.
